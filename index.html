I am an undergraduate student in Mechanical Engineering at Korea Aerospace University, expected to graduate in February 2027.

My research interests lie in autonomous driving and mobile robotics, with a primary emphasis on perception-to-decision pipelines for real-world autonomous systems. I am interested in developing systems that connect visual/LiDAR-based scene understanding, localization, behavior planning, and control on real robotic platforms.

My recent work includes on-device vision-language-model-based robotic mission execution, Dual EKF localization on an ERP42 autonomous vehicle, and real-vehicle autonomous parking. Recently, I have been particularly interested in Vision-Language-Action models for decision making, hallucination-aware task planning, and visual grounding for autonomous driving and mobile robots.

Projects

2025.06 - Present

On-device VLM-based Robotic Mission Execution

Developed a natural-language mission execution framework that converts user commands into executable robot behaviors on a Traxxas TRX-4 UGV platform. The system combines an on-device VLM planner, Actor-Guard-Critic validation, RTAB-Map-based visual SLAM, object detection, structured world states, and ROS2/Nav2 control.

2024.11 - Present

ERP42 Autonomous Driving and Parking

Worked on a real ERP42 autonomous vehicle platform as a member and later team leader of KAUVOY. Implemented Dual EKF localization using IMU, wheel encoder, and RTK-GPS measurements, and developed an autonomous parking pipeline with cone perception, parking-space decision, Hybrid A* path planning, and Pure Pursuit tracking. Currently leading FSM-based decision-making logic for urban driving scenarios.
